Animals & Their Habitats

Evergreen Wildlife Park spans over 5,000 hectares, providing a protected haven for wildlife across multiple ecosystems. From the vast savanna and lush rainforests to the tranquil wetlands and rugged woodlands, every corner of the park offers unique environment where animals thrive.

Among our most fascinating residents are snakes, creatures that play a vital role in maintaining ecological balance. Whether it's the stealthy African Rock Python, the highly venomous Black Mamba, or the elusive Boomslang, these reptiles captivate visitors with their agility and unique survival adaptations

Explore Our Ecosystems

Evergreen Wildlife Park is home to diverse habitats where wildlife thrives. Discover each ecosystem and the animals that depend on them.

Savanna

Expansive grasslands dotted with acacia trees, the savannah is a hotspot for predators and large herbvores.

  • Open grasslands with few trees
  • Seasonal rainfall and dry periods
  • Home to lions, zebras, elephants, cheetahs
  • Herds of herbivores constantly migrate for food

Wetlands

Aquatic ecosystems teeming with life, wetlands support a variety of semi-aquatic and migratory species.

  • Slow-moving rivers, marshes, and swamps
  • Home to hippos, crocodiles, flamingos, and frogs
  • Filters pollutants, maintaining water quality
  • Crucial for bird migration and survival

Rainforest

Dense jungles filled with towering trees, vines, and incredible biodiversity.

  • Dense vegetation with canopy layers
  • High rainfall, creating rich biodiversity
  • Home to monkeys, leopards, toucans, parrots.
  • Important for oxygen product

Snakes

From forests to deserts, snakes adapt to a wide range of environments.

  • Found in diverse environments, from forests to rivers
  • Some species are venomous, while others use constriction
  • Play a crucial role in controlling rodent populations
  • Highly adaptable, thriving in almost every ecosystem
